In Lasalgaon, onions are planted in November, after the rainy season. This means that a healthy supply of onions will arrive in market, during the forthcoming Dec-Jan, when they’re harvested.
In 2008, as expected, there were no rains and this resulted in a healthy quantity of onions being harvested.
Things suddenly took a detour in 2009 and 2010, when unexpected rainfall plagued the lands of Maharashtra. {insert image of water filled lands} Farmers lost their crops and the harvest of onions were significantly low, during the Dec of 2010.
Suddenly, price begins to rise. Ironically reaching an all time high of Rs 2400 per kg.
But is this the only reason for the price rise? With the heavy rains, early harvest and significant reduction of onion yield, some traders began capitalizing on the situation.
Between 2 days, exactly a month apart, in 2010, the same quantity of onions arrived in the markets of Lasalgaon. However, the prices had more than doubled. This is a strong indicator of possible hoarding by traders to control onion prices.
Further, MEP was significantly lowered during the crisis, during March 2011 leading to higher exports and a further shortage of onions in India causing a mayhem.
