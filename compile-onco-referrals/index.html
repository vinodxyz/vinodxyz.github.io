Oncology Network
An analysis of referral patterns in oncology
.. By Shailesh Chitnis, Cyriac Thomas, Arjun and Vinod
Feb 2020
US healthcare system comprises of Integrated Delivery Networks that streamlines cancer treatment by leveraging the power of referrals. Based on the stage and type of cancer, physicians refer patients to specific oncologists to get care and treatment.

Using our health graph data, we've uncovered patterns of referrals within this large network. For this study, we've analyzed the referral patterns for the following set of specializations.



For every physician in the network, whether referring or receiving a referral, we have also identified their primary IDN or group practice.
In 20XX, for a period of 6 months, a total of XX million cancer cases were referred to oncologists (A patient could have multiple cases).
Most of these referrals originated from physicians practicing Internal medicine (tooltip + link), Family medicine, Urology and other classifications.
This is the total outbound referral volume
All of these referrals were inbound to the oncology healthcare system
Nearly 92% of all inbound referrals were for oncologists practising Internal medicine and Radiology.
Referrals actually occur between specialists within each of these areas.
For instance: If we look at Internal medicine, referrals occur from specialists in Gastroenterology, Pulmonary disease etc prominently to oncologists specializing in Hematology & Oncology and Medical Oncology.
However, physicians refer based on an important criteria: The IDN that the oncologist belongs to. Patients are referred to specialists who belong to specific IDNs.
Taking a closer look at Mayo Clinic - one of the IDNs that receives the highest referral volume. We can see that referrals for Mayo Clinic tends to occur across the breadth of US.

Instead, if we look at the top 100 oncologists based on their referral volume. Irrespective of the IDN that they belong to, we find that the same pattern persists.
On a different note, at least 50% of referrals occur within the same IDN. This indicates that a physician is likely to refer an oncologist who belongs to the same IDN.
